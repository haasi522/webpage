About the Temple

The Chennakesava Swamy Temple in Markapur is a historical and architectural marvel from the Vijayanagara Empire, constructed between 1405 and 1459 AD. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, revered as Chennakesava, it is situated on the banks of the Gundlakamma River. The temple is celebrated for its intricate carvings, massive 135-foot Rajagopuram (tower), and musical pillars in the Kalyana Mandapam. A unique phenomenon occurs annually between December and January when the sun's rays at dawn align perfectly to illuminate the deity's feet. Legends state that the temple is named after the demon Kesi's defeat by Lord Vishnu, who is accompanied by Goddess Lakshmi here. It remains a significant spiritual hub, especially during festivals like Ekadasi and Sankranti, drawing thousands of devotees.

Special Rituals(Festivals or Unique Occasions)

Sunlight Ritual (December 16 – January 14)

Time: 6:30 AM – 7:00 AM
Description: During this period, the first rays of the sun directly fall on the deity’s feet in the sanctum.

Kalyanotsavam

Time: Typically between 10:00 AM – 1:00 PM on special festival days.
Description: A symbolic wedding of Lord Chennakesava and Goddess Lakshmi.

Chariot Procession

Time: Evening during festivals (e.g., Ekadasi, Sankranti).
Description: The deity’s Utsav Murti (processional idol) is paraded through the streets.

Abhishekam for Devotees

Time: Usually between 6:30 AM – 9:00 AM (booking required).
